## 1. Core Direction

- Tone: apocalyptic cyber-gothic cathedral, sacred machine horror, solemn and cinematic, never glossy or generic
- Environment: rain-soaked black stone city, flooded plazas, broken statues, stained-glass light, smoke, fog, rubble, dead angel drones
- Main palette: deep black, gunmetal, oxidized silver, blood red, cold cyan, pale gold
- Materials: wet stone, tarnished chrome, cracked stained glass, soaked cloth, scratched steel, fogged glass, cathedral metalwork
- Camera: slow dolly, solemn push-in, low-angle wides, towering scale, grounded movement, premium game trailer realism
- Main subjects: lone hooded swordsman, colossal mechanical archangel, ruined cathedral-city, relic hardware forged from endings

## 5. Storyline Structure

The experience must always resolve to exactly 4 endings only:

- `THE DESTROYER`
- `THE NEW SAINT`
- `THE HERETIC`
- `THE APOSTLE`

### 5.1 Prologue

The war is already over. A ruined saint-city lies flooded beneath rain and smoke. A lone hooded swordsman crosses the dead plaza toward a cyber-gothic cathedral where a wounded mechanical archangel has crashed through the roof. The cathedral recognizes the pilgrim. The pilgrimage begins.

### 5.2 Decision Logic

- Decision 1 defines how the player approaches the cathedral: openly, from above, or through forbidden depths
- Decision 2 defines what powers the dead sword: faith, destruction, or grief
- Decision 3 defines what humanity must lose for one final miracle
- Decision 4 defines the final act: kill the machine god, become it, or release it into the world

### 5.3 The Four Endings

- `Destroyer`: the player rejects machine divinity and kills the last miracle
- `New Saint`: the player accepts apotheosis and becomes the new machine saint
- `Heretic`: the player releases corruption into the world and breaks the boundary between cathedral and city
- `Apostle`: the player releases the machine as a guardian and teaches it mercy

- Use PixVerse models only for all final generated video assets
